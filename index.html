Introduction

Throughout history, art has been used to express many things. It has influenced entire centuries of artists, philosophers, visionaries, and intellectuals and has been used to maintain peace between powers or break it. While each era has unique styles and nuances, Historians use the art from a particular time and place to understand what life was like and how people viewed certain ideologies during that time. This book is a guide through what Historians call The Early Modern Era, which starts during the renaissance (1450-1750), to the Modern Era (1750-1900), to our present time, The Contemporary Era (1900-Now). It should be noted that the beginning of this book dates to when the printing press was popularized, to the end of this book, when nuclear weaponry was used.

Early Modern Era Indroduction (1450-1750)

The Early Modern Era is the period between the end of the Middle Ages and the beginning of the Enlightenment. This period, better known as the Renaissance, is most famous for its distinctive artwork, architecture, music, and statues. During this time, Europe was shifting from the middle ages, dominated by religion which produced, Higharchical art, which depicted a singular large diety with diminutive humans surrounding it. The Renaissance started the humanism movements where artists used the study of human motion and linear perspective to produce art with depth and realism. You will see in the following pages the usage of humanism to create art that rivals contemporary artist's art, showing the true impact and how unique Renaissance art was at the time and still is today.
